What is OnePay? OnePay is a decentralized payment gateway, think a “decentralized Stripe” built on Ethereum. While Stripe processes credit cards, onePay allows any business to process payments in Ethereum and ERC20 tokens.



Why do I need OnePay? Let’s say you want to provide a service in exchange for crypto. For example, let’s say you want to get paid in the stablecoin DAI. You need a way to keep record of customers and orders, to track who paid and for what. OnePay is an application (smart contract) built on Ethereum that allows you to manage your incoming orders: you’ll be able to create a store, add products, set prices, process payments and keep track of the orders received. If you are familiar with a card processor like Stripe, OnePay has many of the same functionalities, but for the crypto world.

How much does OnePay cost? OnePay charges a 0.5% fee on every payment processed. The OnePay application is completely free to use.

Is OnePay safe? The core of OnePay is a open-source, publicly available smart contract running on the Ethereum blockchain. Everyone can independently verify the code, and you can dive deeper into our code base by looking at our technical documentation. OnePay is in beta, so we invite our users to carefully read the docs before implementing the app.

How do I get started? To try OnePay you’ll need to have a web3 browser. If you are accessing OnePay via desktop using Chrome, Metamask is a great option. If you want to use OnePay from you smartphone, check out Opera browser – it is fantastic.
Once your browser is set up, you can start by clicking “Connect” on OnePay homepage. Once you approve OnePay access, you will be guided to create your first “store” on OnePay. A store is a smart contract that OnePay will deploy on the Ethereum blockchain, which will process the incoming payments to your business. You will be able to manage and edit your smart contract from the OnePay interface.
Once your store is up you will be able to add products and set prices for your items. Finally, you will find the instruction to embed the OnePay payment button on your website, by adding just a couple lines of code.

What’s the difference with Coinbase Commerce? OnePay is a fully decentralized application, being essentially just a smart contract running on the Ethereum blockchain. This means it is totally borderless, permissionless and global. Anybody in any part of the world can join the Ethereum network. The same way, anybody can interact with the OnePay smart contract.
Individuals remain responsible for their own use of the protocol against applicable laws in their jurisdiction.

What’s the vision behind OnePay? We want to enable Decentralized Finance to become useful beyond the current speculative use cases. We do this by using the core properties of the Ethereum blockchain (open, decentralized, censorship resistant) to enable a new era of accessible, permissionless commerce at a global scale.
